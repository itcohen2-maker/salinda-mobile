# Hidden Rule Art Direction

## Recommended Direction

Working direction: `Ink Archive`.

This is the strongest midpoint between:

- premium puzzle
- scholarly mystery
- tactile tabletop materials
- enough boldness to avoid generic mobile game UI

The interface should feel like a quiet instrument on a dark archive desk, not like a casino table and not like a fantasy RPG menu.

## Core Mood

- calm
- intelligent
- deliberate
- slightly secretive
- tactile
- editorial rather than playful

## Visual Rules

1. The cards stay central.
2. Color appears on meaning, not everywhere.
3. Brass is reserved for hierarchy and confirmation.
4. Surfaces are matte, not glossy.
5. Ornament is sparse and structural.
6. Empty space is part of the style.

## Palette

Base neutrals:

- `#14161B` background charcoal
- `#1C2128` deep surface
- `#232A33` raised surface
- `#2F3A4A` ink blue

Text and material:

- `#EDE4D3` ivory
- `#CFC6B8` soft text
- `#A88A58` soft brass
- `#7B6541` dark brass

Reserved accents:

- `#6A3434` oxblood
- `#5F6B63` muted green-gray

## Background Strategy

Gameplay background:

- deep charcoal base
- subtle ink-blue radial lift behind the play area
- soft vignette at the edges
- optional ultra-light paper or dust texture

Chapter intro background:

- darker center staging
- more atmospheric falloff
- slightly warmer brass/oxblood presence

Rules or meta screens:

- flatter, quieter surface
- less dramatic contrast
- slightly more readable text field

## Button Hierarchy

- `Primary`: brass-framed plaque
- `Secondary`: inscribed dark panel with ivory hairline
- `Premium`: ceremonial plaque, used sparingly

## Typography Direction

- one readable serif family across English and Hebrew
- no playful rounded game fonts
- no uppercase-only dependency for core actions

## Motion Direction

- idle is mostly still
- press states should feel tactile
- reveals should feel deliberate
- no sparkle loops
- no noisy ambient animation

## What To Avoid

- casino green felt as the dominant background
- high-gloss pill buttons
- neon edges
- purple fantasy cliches
- noisy textures
- crowded HUD framing

## Alternate Directions

### 1. Cold Editorial Occult

Cleaner, flatter, more typographic.
Best if the product should feel bolder and more modern.

### 2. Brass Instrument

More mechanical and ritualized.
Best if the interface should feel like an antique measuring device.

### 3. Oxblood Chamber

Darker and more theatrical.
Use carefully; it can become too dramatic too quickly.

## Current Recommendation

Keep `Ink Archive` as the base direction.
It is distinctive without becoming costume-like.
